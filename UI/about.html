About Us

The primary aim of participating in eTutor for your students is to provide them with an authentic reason for reading and writing in English. For Victorian schools, participation in eTutor also has the potential to address the AUSVELS general capability of Intercultural Understanding and the cross-curriculum priority of Asia and Australia's engagement with Asia.

eTutor is primarily about encouraging English reading and writing skills. However, in the 21st century, we cannot ignore the role that digital technologies are playing in education around the world. So, in eTutor we encourage the students to make use of digital media as an alternative to writing in text.

What We Do

E-tutoring is a challenging activity: e-tutors often have a range of duties they must carry out and often the workload in e-tutoring can be intense. In this activity, you are going to consider the variety of tasks which an e-tutor may need to undertake as part of their teaching, and then categorise them according to the kind of tutoring role that they reflect: social, organisational, or pedagogical/intellectual.
